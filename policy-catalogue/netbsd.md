## Verbatim excerpt
> "Code generated by a large language model or similar technology, such as GitHub/Microsoft's Copilot, OpenAI's ChatGPT, or Facebook/Meta's Code Llama, is presumed to be tainted code, and must not be committed without prior written approval by core."

## Notes
The "presumed tainted + approval exception" construction is distinct from both a flat ban (QEMU/servo/Gentoo) and disclosed-allowed (LLVM/Ghostty/curl): it's **prohibited-unless-escalated**. v0.2 signal: the stance vocabulary needs an `exception-pathway` concept — who can grant it and how — because at least one major project's policy is exactly that.
